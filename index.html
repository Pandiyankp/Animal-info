ANIMAL INFO

LION

the king of the jungle
Lion is a powerful wild animal that lives in the forest.

TIGER

The national animal of India.
Tiger is a very intelligent and powerful animal

FOX

Foxes are members of the dog family
A group of foxes is called a “skulk”

MONKEY

Monkey is a common name that may refer to most mammals of the infraorder Simiiformes Monkeys are generally considered to be intelligent, especially the Old World monkeys.

WOLF

Wolves live and hunt in packs of around six to ten animals. They are known to roam large distances, perhaps 12 miles in a single day.

LEOPARD

Leopards are graceful and powerful big cats closely related to lions, tigers, and jaguars. They live in sub-Saharan Africa, north east Africa, Central Asia, India, and China.

GORILLA

Gorillas are herbivorous, predominantly ground-dwelling great apes that inhabit the tropical forests of equatorial Africa. Gorillas tend to live 35-40 years in the wild.

PANDA

The giant panda, also known as the panda bear, is a bear native to South Central China. It is characterised by its bold black-and-white coat and rotund body.

DEER

Deer is a wild animal that belongs to the Cervidae family. Deers have about 50 species such as the reindeer, red deer, elk, white-tailed deer, and moose among others.

HIPPOPOTAMUS

hippopotamus, also called the hippo, common hippopotamus or river hippopotamus, is a large, mostly herbivorous, semiaquatic mammal and ungulate native to sub-Saharan Africa

BISON

Bison are the largest mammal in North America. Male bison (called bulls) weigh up to 2,000 pounds and stand 6 feet tall, while females (called cows) weigh up to 1,000 pounds and reach a height of 4-5 feet. Bison calves weigh 30-70 pounds at birth. Bison at Rocky Mountain Arsenal National Wildlife Refuge in Colorado

RHINOCEROS

Rhinoceroses are large, herbivorous mammals identified by their characteristic horned snouts. The word "rhinoceros" comes from the Greek "rhino" (nose) and "ceros" (horn). ... Their horns are sometimes sold as trophies or decorations, but more often they are ground up and used in traditional Chinese medicine
